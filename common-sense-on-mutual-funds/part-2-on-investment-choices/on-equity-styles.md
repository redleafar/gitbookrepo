In investing, "value" refers to companies trading below their intrinsic worth, often with lower price-to-earnings (P/E) ratios, while "growth" refers to companies with high growth potential and earnings, typically trading at higher valuations. Value investors seek to buy undervalued assets that they believe will rebound, while growth investors focus on future growth and potential, even if the current price seems high
